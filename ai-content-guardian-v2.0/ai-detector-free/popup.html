üö® Common Email Scams:

‚Ä¢ Urgency/Threats: "Act now or your account will be closed!"
‚Ä¢ Suspicious Links: Links that don't match the company name
‚Ä¢ Prize/Money Scams: "You've won!" or "Claim your refund"
‚Ä¢ Impersonation: Pretending to be your bank, IRS, Amazon, etc.
‚Ä¢ Personal Info Requests: Asking for passwords, SSN, card numbers
‚Ä¢ Poor Grammar: Lots of spelling and grammar mistakes
‚Ä¢ Generic Greetings: "Dear Customer" instead of your name

‚úÖ Safety Tips:

  1. Never click links in suspicious emails - Type the website directly in your browser
  2. If it's about your bank: Call the number on the back of your card (NOT the number in the email)
  3. If it's about a package: Check the tracking directly on the shipping company's website
  4. If it mentions the IRS/CRA: They NEVER contact you by email first
  5. Check the sender's email address carefully - scammers use look-alike addresses
  6. When in doubt, ask a trusted family member or friend
  7. Never send money or gift cards based on an email request
